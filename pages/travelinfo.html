Mauritius Travel Info

VISA INFORMATION

Tourist Visa

Citizens from many countries, including the EU, USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand, can enter Mauritius without a visa for short stays (usually up to 90 days). Check the exact duration and requirements based on your nationality on the official Mauritius immigration website or through your local Mauritian embassy.

Visa on Arrival

Some nationalities can obtain a visa on arrival for tourism purposes. Ensure you have a return ticket, proof of accommodation, and sufficient funds for your stay.

-Pre-Arranged Visa-

Countries Requiring a Visa

If your country is not on the visa-free or visa-on-arrival list, you'll need to apply for a visa before traveling. Contact the nearest Mauritian embassy or consulate for details on the application process.

-Required Documentation-

Passport:

Must be valid for at least six months beyond your planned departure date from Mauritius.

Return/Onward Ticket:

Proof of a return or onward journey is often required.

Accommodation:

Confirmation of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter from a host, etc.).

Proof of Sufficient Funds:

Evidence that you have enough money to cover your stay (bank statements, credit card, etc.).

Health Insurance:

While not mandatory, itâ€™s advisable to have travel health insurance covering your stay.

-Customs Regulations-

Duty-Free Allowance:

Visitors can bring in a limited quantity of duty-free goods, including alcohol and tobacco. Check the latest allowances to avoid fines.

Prohibited Items:

Importing certain items, such as narcotics, firearms, and indecent material, is prohibited. Check the Mauritian Customs website for a complete list.

-Additional Tips-

Local Registration:

If staying for an extended period, you might need to register with the local authorities.

Driving License:

Tourists can drive with their national driving license for up to four weeks. An International Driving Permit (IDP) might be required for longer stays.

Emergency Contacts

Police: 112
Ambulance: 114
Fire Department: 115

THE ALL IN ONE FORM

**Travel Requirements to Mauritius**
Before traveling, all visitors are advised to complete the Mauritius All in One travel form, accessible via the QR Code. This form will generate a PDF document with a QR code. Make sure to print a hard copy of the completed form and present it to Health Officials upon arrival at Mauritius Airport. If you have not completed the All in One travel form, foreign nationals will need to fill out an Immigration Disembarkation Card upon arrival at the airport. Remember to fill out the All in One Travel form and bring a printed copy with you.
