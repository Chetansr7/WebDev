Language in Mauritius

Mauritius is a linguistically diverse island where multiple languages coexist harmoniously.

The most commonly spoken language, however, is Mauritian Creole, a French-based creole that serves as the lingua franca for the majority of the population. This multilingualism reflects the island's rich cultural heritage and history of immigration.

Other languages spoken in Mauritius are:

French | English | Urdu | Arabic | Bhojpuri | Hindi | Tamil | Telegu | Marathi | Mandarin


English: Official language used in government, legal documents, and education.

French: Widely spoken in media, business, and daily interactions.

Other Languages: Used within specific communities and cultural practices.


To help you get acquainted with the local way of speaking, here are some common phrases used in Mauritian Creole, French, and English:

Greetings

Good morning

Mauritian Creole: "Bonzour"

French: "Bonjour"

Good evening

Mauritian Creole: "Bonswar"

French: "Bonsoir"

Good night

Mauritian Creole: "Bon nwit"

French: "Bonne nuit"

Courtesy

Please

Mauritian Creole: "Silvouple"

French: "S'il vous plaît"

You're welcome

Mauritian Creole: "Corec sa"

French: "De rien"

Excuse me / Sorry

Mauritian Creole: "Eskiz mwa"

French: "Excusez-moi"

Common Questions

What's your name?

Mauritian Creole: "Ki to nom?"

French: "Comment tu t'appelles?"

Where are you from?

Mauritian Creole: "Kot to sorti?"

French: "D'où viens-tu?"

How much is this?

Mauritian Creole: "Komien sa?"

French: "Combien ça coûte?"

Directions

Where is...?

Mauritian Creole: "Kot eter...?"

French: "Où est...?"

Left

Mauritian Creole: "Gos"

French: "Gauche"

Right

Mauritian Creole: "Drwat"

French: "Droite"

Straight ahead

Mauritian Creole: "Tou drwat"

French: "Tout droit"

Daily Expressions

I'm hungry

Mauritian Creole: "Mo faim"

French: "J'ai faim"

I'm thirsty

Mauritian Creole: "Mo swaf"

French: "J'ai soif"

I don't understand

Mauritian Creole: "Mo pa konpran"

French: "Je ne comprends pas"

Miscellaneous
