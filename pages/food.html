Food in Mauritius

Mauritian cuisine is a tantalising fusion of flavours, influenced by the island's diverse cultural heritage. From the aromatic spices of Indian curries to the savoury flavours of Chinese stir-fries and the rich, hearty dishes of French and Creole origins, every meal is a celebration of the island's multicultural roots. Street food is a must-try, with popular snacks like dholl puri, samosas, and gateau piment offering a burst of flavours in every bite. Fresh seafood, tropical fruits, and locally-grown vegetables add to the vibrant and varied culinary landscape, making every dining experience in Mauritius a delightful adventure for the taste buds.

Mauritian Favourites: A Feast for the Senses

Dholl Puri
Mauritius's National Dish, is a spiced flatbread filled with yellow split peas and served with chutney and curry.
Gato Piment
It is a popular Mauritian snack made from spicy, deep-fried lentil cakes. These savoury treats are seasoned with herbs and spices.
Dupain Frire
Fried bread, a popular Mauritian snack. Slices of bread are deep-fried until crispy and often served with sachini.
A man selling oily cakes from his motorcycle, offering freshly fried treats to local neighbourhoods.
Merveille
A crispy, deep-fried Mauritian snack made from a simple dough of flour, butter, and salt.
A typical Mauritian street food stand offers delicious snacks like dholl puri, samoosas and a variety of curries served with rice or bread.
Confi
A treat, typically includes candied fruits and vegetables like mango, pineapple, and tamarind, preserved in sugar and spices. The vendor rides through neighbourhoods, offering these sweet and tangy delights from the back of his bike,
Du pain tikka
It is a a flavourful chicken mixture with cheese, then deep-fried until crispy. It is served with bread and several sauces.
Boulette
In Mauritius are small dumplings made from minced meat, spices, and sometimes vegetables. They are steamed .
Mine bouille
It is a fried noodle dish, often stir-fried with vegetables and chicken, meat or seafood, seasoned with spices.
Mine frire
It refers to stir-fried noodleswhich is cooked with vegetables and chicken, meat or seafood, seasoned with soy sauce and spices for a savoury flavour.
Bol renversé
A Mauritian dish of stir-fried rice with vegetables and meat, topped with a sunny-side-up egg. It is served by flipping the bowl onto the plate.
Biryani
Mauritian Biryani consists of rice, meat ( chicken or beef), and potatoes. It is known for its rich flavours and aromatic spices.
Poisson salé
Salted fish, typically dried and cured to preserve it. It is commonly served with rice and often accompanied by vegetables or a spicy sauce. The saltiness of the fish complements the mild flavour of the rice, creating a simple yet flavourful meal.
Duriz frire
A Mauritian dish of fried rice. It is made by stir-frying pre-cooked rice with vegetables, meat, or seafood, and seasoned with soy sauce and spices.
7 cari
It is a meal with seven different curry dishes, often including a mix of vegetables, and lentils, served together to showcase diverse flavours, mostly during Hindu weddings.
A typical well-rounded Mauritian dish served with grilled chicken, saussages, bread and salad.
Grillade
A Mauritian dish of grilled or pan-fried meat, typically beef or lamb, seasoned with spices and herbs. It is known for its rich, smoky flavour and is often served with accompaniments like rice, vegetables, or a spicy sauce. The meat is usually marinated before cooking to enhance its taste.
Coconut water
It is a popular and refreshing drink. It is commonly enjoyed straight from the coconut, served chilled, especially in tropical settings or at street stalls.
Letchi
Known as lychee, is a tropical fruit popular in Mauritius. It has a bumpy, reddish skin that is peeled away to reveal a translucent, juicy flesh with a sweet, floral flavour. Letchi is enjoyed fresh, often chilled, and is also used in desserts, fruit salads, and beverages.
Tarte banane
A Mauritian dessert made with ripe bananas. It typically features a sweet, custard-like filling made from mashed bananas, sugar, and eggs, baked in a pastry crust.
Gato arouille
A Mauritian snack made from grated taro. The grated taro is mixed with spices, herbs, and sometimes a bit of flour, then shaped into small cakes or fritters and deep-fried until crispy.
Corn pudding
A dessert made from cornmeal. It has a smooth, creamy texture and is often flavoured with vanilla, spices, or fruits. The pudding is cooked until it thickens, then chilled and served.
Napolitaine
A sweet cake, often a layered dessert with a soft, spongy texture. It might be flavoured with ingredients like vanilla or fruit, and sometimes includes icing or a glaze.
The Kulfi Dreams ice cream is a local variation, combining the rich, creamy texture of kulfi with tropical Mauritian ingredients.
Mauritian ice cream often features unique flavors that reflect the island's tropical and multicultural influences.
At the Bazaar Port Louis, you can find a vibrant array of fruits and vegetables reflecting the diverse produce of Mauritius.
The bazaar is a lively and colourful market, where you can find both local produce and a mix of exotic fruits and vegetables, reflecting the island’s rich culinary diversity.
