Frequently Asked Questions

-INSURANCE QUESTIONS-

Do I need insurance to travel to Mauritius?

Travel insurance is advisable but not mandatory at this time.

– GUEST EXPERIENCE –

Will guests need to wear a mask?

You do not need to wear a face mask during your holiday. It is only recommended in airports, during airport transfers and on public transport. It is also recommended in healthcare settings, such as hospitals and pharmacies. It is mandatory to wear a mask if you have tested positive for COVID-19. We also recommend wearing a facemask if you are over 60 years of age, if you are immunocompromised or have comorbidities, or if you are in a very confined space.

Are there family-friendly activities in Mauritius?

Yes, Mauritius is a family-friendly destination with numerous activities for children and adults alike. These include water parks, nature reserves, boat tours, and family-friendly resorts with kids' clubs.

What are some must-try local dishes?

Some must-try local dishes include:
Dholl Puri: Flatbread filled with ground split peas.
Rougaille: A tomato-based sauce often served with fish or meat.
Gâteau Piment: Spicy lentil fritters.
Fresh seafood, particularly grilled fish and shellfish.

– CHILDREN AND FAMILIES –

Will children need to do COVID testing?

No. The rules for travellers, regardless of vaccination status, are the same. You do not need to do COVID-19 tests.

Do children need to be vaccinated?

No. The rules for travellers, regardless of vaccination status or age, are the same. You do not need to be vaccinated to enter Mauritius. It is recommended that children over 5 years old, as well as adults, wear a mask at ports, airports, during airport transfers and on public transport. A mask is also recommended in healthcare settings, including hospitals and pharmacies.

Will kids’ and teens’ clubs and babysitting services be available?

Access will be allowed to the kids/teens’ club in compliance with sanitary protocols, with mandatory closing time during the day for full disinfection and compulsory pre-registration. The number of children will be limited at all times (with a defined maximum number of children per staff member) with a preference for outdoor activities. Babysitting service is allowed, provided that the babysitter and his/her immediate family members living in the same household have received two doses of COVID-19 vaccine at least 14 days beforehand (28 days if a Johnson & Johnson vaccine).

-GENERAL QUESTIONS-

Is Mauritius safe for tourists?

Mauritius is generally considered a safe destination for tourists. However, as with any travel destination, it is advisable to take standard precautions such as avoiding isolated areas at night, keeping valuables secure, and being aware of your surroundings.

What currency is used in Mauritius?

The currency used in Mauritius is the Mauritian Rupee (MUR). It is advisable to carry some local currency for small purchases, though credit cards are widely accepted in hotels, restaurants, and larger stores.

What is the best time to visit Mauritius?

The best time to visit Mauritius is during the cooler, dry months from May to December. The weather is pleasant, and there is less humidity, making it ideal for outdoor activities and exploring the island.

What should I wear in Mauritius?

Lightweight clothing suitable for tropical weather is ideal, especially breathable fabrics. If you plan to visit religious sites or formal venues, modest attire is appreciated. Don't forget sunscreen and a hat for protection from the sun.

What is the cuisine like in Mauritius?

Mauritian cuisine is a delightful blend of Indian, Chinese, French, and Creole influences. Popular dishes include dholl puri (a type of flatbread), seafood curries, rougaille (tomato-based sauce), and various tropical fruits.

What is the capital of Mauritius?

The capital of Mauritius is Port Louis.

What is the main religion in Mauritius?

Mauritius is a multicultural society with several religions. Hinduism is the largest religion, followed by Christianity, Islam, and Buddhism.

What type of electrical outlets are used in Mauritius?

Mauritius uses Type C and Type G electrical outlets. The standard voltage is 230 V, and the standard frequency is 50 Hz.
